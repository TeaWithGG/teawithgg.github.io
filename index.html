About

There is a kind of person
who walks into a room
and immediately reads it.

I have always been that person. I just spent years learning how to use it.

I grew up between Amritsar and London — two cities, two cultures, two entirely different ways of seeing. Somewhere in that distance I developed something that can't be taught: the instinct to pay attention when everyone else is performing.

A debate judge — someone whose job was to find weakness — told me I had the precision of a natural anchor and the instincts of a real journalist. I didn't celebrate. I got to work.

I'm a journalist interning at The Tribune, a cinematographer who directed her first two works entirely solo, a photographer who shoots in silence, a public speaker who has held stages, and a visual storyteller who believes every frame, every word, and every room has a story waiting to be told right.

My cinematography is rich, decadent, raw and uncut — soaked in culture, colour and history. I am not confined to one role. I am fluid — and that is my greatest strength.

The Journey

Early Years
🇬🇧
London & The Art of Attention

Four years in a city that taught me to see the world differently. Between two cultures, I developed something that can't be taught — the instinct to pay attention.

Growing up between London and Amritsar gave me two lenses. One that sees with the precision of distance. One that sees with the warmth of belonging. Both live in everything I create.
2022 — 2023
🎤
The Debate Stage

A judge told me I'd make a great anchor. I walked away with two first prizes in impromptu speech and an appreciation award from the Police Commissionerate of Amritsar.

1st Prize — Prantiya Sanskriti Mahotsav. 2nd Prize — Uttar Shetr Sanskriti Mahotsav. Appreciation Award — Speech on Shaheed Bhagat Singh's Birthday. The stage has never intimidated me. It has always felt like home.
2023 — 2024
📸
The First Frame

I picked up a camera and discovered I'd been seeing in macro all along. Every shot an emotion. Every frame a decision. Beauty doesn't announce itself — it waits.

My photography is 90% macro — the world in extreme close-up. A bee on a mustard flower. The universe inside a human iris. I don't hunt for beauty. I wait for it to reveal itself.
2026
🎬
Dhagar Dhun & Antim Darpan

Two complete original works — one documentary film, one radio drama — both directed, produced, scripted, and edited entirely by me. Solo. From first word to final cut.

Dhagar Dhun — a 7-minute documentary portrait of an all-women film crew set against Jammu's Dogri culture. Every light placed by hand. Every cut intentional. Antim Darpan followed — a radio drama built on sound alone. Two formats. One commitment to craft.
February 2026
🎪
Sacred Amritsar

Hundreds of delegates. An international cultural festival. Artists including Usha Uthup and Kailash Kher. I personally managed 13+ delegates — and by the final evening, every one of them asked for me by name.

Teamwork Arts. Sacred Amritsar. 72 hours of logistics, hospitality, crisis management, and human connection. I used to doubt my ability to give people a satisfactory experience. Sacred Amritsar erased that doubt permanently. I thrive under pressure.
2025 — Present
🗞️
The Tribune

The newsroom. The real thing. Currently interning at one of India's most respected national newspapers — learning the discipline, ethics, and craft of journalism from the ground up.

Every day in this newsroom is a masterclass. Research, drafting, editorial coordination, fact-checking, story development. I am building the foundation of a career I intend to be remarkable.
