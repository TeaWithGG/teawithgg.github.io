About Me

Between every story.
Fully present.

I grew up between two worlds — Amritsar and London — and somewhere between two cultures and two ways of seeing, I found that the only thing I was always doing was paying attention.

At family events, I was the one talking. Holding the room. Making sure the right information reached the right people at the right moment. A debate judge once told me I had a fact-oriented, no-nonsense style and that I'd make a great anchor. I went home and decided to take that seriously.

"I capture silence. I capture emotion. My lens doesn't perform — it witnesses."

I'm a journalist, cinematographer, photographer, public speaker, and visual storyteller currently in my third year of Journalism and Mass Communication at BBK DAV Women's College, Amritsar. I intern at The Tribune, I've managed 13 international delegates at Sacred Amritsar for Teamwork Arts, and I've directed, produced, and edited my own documentary film entirely solo.

My cinematography is rich, decadent, raw and uncut — soaked in culture, colour and history. I am not confined to one role. I never have been. I am fluid — and that is my greatest strength.
