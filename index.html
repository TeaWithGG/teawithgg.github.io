About

Between two cities.
Inside every story.

I grew up in London for four years — the years that shape you most. Somewhere between two cultures, two cities, and two ways of seeing the world, I found that the only thing I was always doing was paying attention.

At family events, I was the one talking. Holding the room. Making sure the right information reached the right people at the right moment. A debate judge once told me I had a fact-oriented, no-nonsense style and that I'd make a great anchor. I went home and decided to take that seriously.

I'm Gungun Arora — a journalist, cinematographer, and visual storyteller based in Amritsar. I shoot moments most people walk past. A mother lulling her child to sleep. A man bowing his head to something greater than himself, asking to be seen by a merciful eye. Two friends sharing a popsicle on the way home. I capture silence. I capture emotion.

My cinematography is rich, decadent, raw and uncut — soaked in culture, colour and history. At Sacred Amritsar, I managed 13 international delegates over three days — and by day three, every single one of them told my supervisor I was the best delegate manager they had ever encountered. I am not confined to one role. I never have been. I am fluid — and that is my greatest strength.
